# RBI Fair Practices Code & Lending Guidelines

## Fair Practices Code (FPC) — RBI Master Direction 2024

### Disclosure Requirements
- Lenders MUST disclose the **all-inclusive interest rate** (annual percentage rate) before disbursement.
- All fees, charges, and penalties must be listed upfront in the loan agreement.
- The loan agreement must be in a language understood by the borrower.

### EMI Calculation
- EMI must be calculated on a **reducing balance basis** (not flat rate). This is mandated by RBI circular DBOD.No.Dir.BC.56/13.03.00/2003-04.
- Flat rate interest is deceptive — a 12% flat rate on a 3-year loan is actually ~21% reducing balance.
- Borrowers should always ask: "Yeh reducing balance pe hai ya flat rate pe?"

### Prepayment Rules
- **No prepayment penalty** on floating rate loans (RBI circular 2014).
- Fixed rate loans may have prepayment charges, but these must be disclosed upfront.
- Borrowers have the right to prepay any loan and save on interest.

### Loan Rejection
- Banks MUST provide reasons in writing if a loan application is rejected.
- Borrowers can approach the Banking Ombudsman if treated unfairly.

## Microfinance Institution (MFI) Guidelines — RBI 2022

### Interest Rate Cap
- Total cost of borrowing for MFI loans must be disclosed as an all-inclusive APR.
- MFIs cannot charge hidden processing fees, insurance premiums, or documentation charges separately.
- RBI removed the interest rate cap in 2022, but requires board-approved pricing policy.

### FOIR Guidelines
- **FOIR (Fixed Obligation to Income Ratio)** should not exceed 50% for MFI loans.
- This means EMI + other fixed obligations should be ≤ 50% of monthly household income.
- If FOIR > 50%, the loan is risky and should ideally not be given.

### Loan Limits
- For households with annual income ≤ ₹3,00,000: classified as microfinance.
- Multiple lending: total indebtedness of MFI borrower should not exceed ₹1,25,000.
- Not more than 3 MFIs should lend to the same borrower.

## Moneylender (Sahukaar) Facts

### Legal Framework
- Moneylending is regulated by state-level Moneylenders Acts.
- Many states cap interest at 12-18% per annum; many moneylenders violate this.
- Interest rates of 30-60% charged by informal moneylenders are technically illegal in most states.

### Warning Signs of Predatory Lending
- Interest calculated on "flat rate" instead of reducing balance
- No written agreement or receipt
- Threats or intimidation for recovery
- Taking personal belongings as "collateral" (jewelry, Aadhaar card, ration card)
- Compounding interest monthly ("byaaj pe byaaj")

### What to Do if Trapped
1. Report to local police — harassment for debt recovery is illegal (IPC Section 506)
2. Approach the District Magistrate or SDM
3. Contact legal aid: NALSA (National Legal Services Authority) — free legal help
4. Contact M3M Foundation or local NGO for counselling

- अगर कोई लोन देने से मना करे तो लिखित में कारण माँगें।
- साहूकार से 30-60% ब्याज लेना ज़्यादातर राज्यों में ग़ैरक़ानूनी है।
- अगर कोई धमकी दे तो पुलिस में रिपोर्ट करें — कर्ज़ वसूली के लिए डराना IPC 506 के तहत अपराध है।
- FOIR (EMI / आमदनी) 50% से ज़्यादा नहीं होना चाहिए।
