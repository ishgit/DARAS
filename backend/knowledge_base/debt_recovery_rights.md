# Debt Recovery & Legal Rights — Borrower Protection

## Your Rights as a Borrower

### RBI Guidelines on Recovery
- Banks and NBFCs CANNOT use threatening or abusive language
- Recovery agents must carry authorization letter and ID card
- Calls only between 7 AM and 7 PM — no late-night harassment
- No visiting your workplace to embarrass you
- No disclosing your loan details to neighbours or family (privacy violation)

### Illegal Practices by Moneylenders
- Taking Aadhaar card, ration card, or voter ID as "collateral" — ILLEGAL
- Charging interest on interest (compound interest without agreement) — ILLEGAL in most states
- Threatening physical harm for non-payment — CRIMINAL OFFENSE (IPC 506)
- Seizing personal property without court order — ILLEGAL
- Forcing you to sign blank papers — ILLEGAL

### What To Do If Harassed
1. **File FIR:** Go to nearest police station. Loan recovery harassment is a criminal offense.
2. **Banking Ombudsman:** For banks/NBFCs — file complaint at cms.rbi.org.in
3. **District Consumer Forum:** For unfair trade practices
4. **NALSA:** National Legal Services Authority provides FREE lawyers for economically weak
5. **State Legal Services Authority (SLSA):** Free legal aid at district level

## Loan Restructuring Options
If you cannot pay EMI, you have legal options:
1. **Moratorium:** Request bank to pause EMI for 1-6 months
2. **Loan Restructuring:** Extend tenure to reduce EMI
3. **One-Time Settlement (OTS):** Negotiate to pay a lump sum less than total outstanding
4. **Debt Consolidation:** Combine multiple loans into one with lower interest
